Neon Odessey

Multi-Sensory Design, TouchDesigner, p5js — 2024

This project began with a technical exploration of Arduino and p5.js, focusing on how physical inputs could be translated into dynamic digital outputs. Early experiments involved working with buttons, light sensors (LDR), and potentiometers to understand how data could be captured, processed, and visualised in real time . These exercises established the foundation for interaction, where simple inputs, such as pressing a button or adjusting light, could directly influence visual behaviours on screen. This initial phase was crucial in bridging hardware and software, allowing us to think beyond static outputs and towards responsive, interactive systems.

Through collaborative brainstorming, the project evolved into Neon Odyssey, a speculative, immersive concept that transforms a miniature box into a kaleidoscopic club experience. As seen in page 6, the idea emerged from combining references of light installations, disco environments, and kaleidoscope visuals into a single interactive artefact. The system was designed around dual states: a “day” mode featuring a DVD screensaver-like visual, and a “night” mode that activates vibrant kaleidoscopic patterns and audio when the light sensor is blocked and the button is pressed. This interaction was reinforced physically through a black box with a peephole, reflective interiors, and miniature elements, creating a layered spatial experience that merges digital visuals with tangible form.


The final outcome presents a fully integrated interactive installation, where users engage with both physical and digital components simultaneously. As shown in the final setup, the experience is activated through simple gestures, covering the sensor and pressing a button, triggering a transformation from mundane to immersive. Despite challenges in coding precision and physical construction, the project demonstrates how input, processing, and output can be seamlessly combined to create an engaging sensory experience. Ultimately, Neon Odyssey explores how small-scale interactions can produce moments of escapism, turning a simple box into a dynamic, playful environment that blurs the line between reality and simulation.
