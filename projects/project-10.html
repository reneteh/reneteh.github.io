Bodiscover

Advertising Campaign, Visual Storytelling — 2022

This project began with an investigation into Body Dysmorphic Disorder (BDD), focusing on how societal pressures and unrealistic beauty standards shape self-perception. Research revealed that BDD is often under-recognised, with individuals becoming overly fixated on perceived flaws due to influences from social media, peer comparison, and cultural expectations . Surveys and user insights further highlighted how common these insecurities are, particularly among young adults, reinforcing the need for a more engaging and accessible way to raise awareness. This led to the core intention of the project: to create a platform that not only educates but also encourages empathy and self-reflection.

The process developed into a multi-platform campaign titled BODISCOVER, built around the idea of “discovering” one’s body beyond distorted standards. Through persona building and journey mapping, the project identified key behavioural patterns—such as comparison, self-doubt, and social media influence, that informed the design direction. This translated into a campaign system that spans pre-launch, launch, and post-launch phases, integrating touchpoints such as social media content, interactive filters, a dedicated website, and outdoor advertisements featuring distorted mirrors. The use of bright, playful visuals paired with distortion became a key strategy, creating a contrast that draws attention while reflecting the underlying issue of warped self-perception.

The final outcome presents BODISCOVER as an experiential awareness campaign centred around participation and engagement. The main activation, an adventure hunt event, invites users to complete missions related to body positivity, supported by physical artefacts such as booklets, goodie bags, and interactive installations. This is extended through digital platforms, including social media storytelling, filters, and a website that facilitates education, registration, and advocacy. By combining physical experiences with digital engagement, the project moves beyond passive awareness, encouraging users to actively reflect, empathise, and contribute to reshaping perceptions of beauty and self-worth.
