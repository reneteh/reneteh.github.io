Go to Hell

Illustration App Design, UIUX — 2021

This project began with an exploration of Haw Par Villa as a culturally rich yet often overlooked heritage site in Singapore. While the park is known for its vivid depictions of Chinese mythology and the Ten Courts of Hell, research revealed that younger audiences often avoid visiting due to perceptions of it being either too frightening or visually overwhelming. This insight shaped the direction of the project, to reinterpret Haw Par Villa as an engaging and accessible experience, using design to bridge the gap between traditional narratives and contemporary audiences.

Through iterative development, the project evolved into a mobile application concept that combines storytelling, interaction, and education. The design process involved structuring key user flows such as onboarding, homepage navigation, and content discovery, ensuring users could easily explore the space without feeling lost. As seen in the presentation board, features such as events and articles provide contextual learning, while the map and augmented reality functions allow users to interact directly with the statues, transforming them into animated or illustrated forms. This approach reframes the site from a passive viewing experience into an active journey, where users engage with cultural content through multiple layers of interaction.
