By Chance - Tinder

UIUX, Experience-Driven Innovation — 2025

This project began with a critical exploration of how dating apps shape human interaction, particularly how platforms like Tinder often prioritise speed and visual appeal over meaningful connection. Through competitor analysis, campaign studies, and a public survey of over 100 users, we identified a recurring issue: while dating apps are easy to use, they frequently result in superficial interactions and a lack of genuine emotional connection . This insight became the foundation of our investigation, pushing us to question how digital design could move beyond passive swiping into something more intentional and human.

The process involved multiple stages of refinement, from defining a clear problem statement to mapping user journeys and building personas grounded in real experiences. One of the main challenges was narrowing down broad insights into a focused direction, as many of the issues identified were not unique to Tinder alone. Through iterative feedback and reframing using the “How Might We” method, we shifted our focus toward creating moments of authentic interaction rather than redesigning the entire system. This led to extensive ideation and prototyping, where we explored different concepts such as anonymous matching, text-based speed dating, and real-time interactions before converging on a solution that balanced spontaneity with usability.

The final outcome, By Chance, introduces a spontaneous video-call feature that connects users in real time, encouraging unscripted and organic conversations. Unlike traditional swiping, this feature creates a sense of unpredictability, users receive a match at unexpected moments and have a limited time to engage, simulating the feeling of meeting someone naturally. The design retains Tinder’s familiar interface while reimagining how connections are initiated, supported by a broader campaign that emphasises serendipity and emotional storytelling. Ultimately, the project reflects a shift from designing for efficiency to designing for experience, highlighting how intentional moments of interaction can transform digital relationships into something more meaningful.
