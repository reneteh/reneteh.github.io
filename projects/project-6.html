Fridge App

UIUX, Digital Product Design — 2025

This project began with the design of a smart refrigerator app prototype, focusing on how digital interfaces can improve everyday routines through intuitive UI/UX. Initial research into existing apps such as Hitachi and LG ThinQ revealed key issues, cluttered navigation, poor hierarchy, and inefficient access to essential features . These insights shaped the core direction of the project: to create a user-centered system that prioritises clarity, accessibility, and ease of use. The aim was not just to include multiple features, but to structure them in a way that aligns with how users naturally interact with their appliances.

Through sketching and wire-framing, the app was broken down into key sections, homepage, food storage, control panel, and settings, each designed around specific user needs and behaviours. As seen in the early sketches, the homepage consolidates essential information such as temperature and expiring food, while the food stock section allows users to monitor items by category and expiry. Iterations focused heavily on improving information hierarchy, simplifying navigation, and refining visual consistency. The design evolved from icon-heavy layouts to more image-based interfaces, creating a more intuitive and relatable experience when identifying food items. This iterative process highlighted the importance of balancing functionality with visual clarity, ensuring the interface remained both efficient and engaging.

The final outcome is a high-fidelity interactive prototype built in Figma, integrating features such as food tracking, temperature control, water level monitoring, and real-time notifications. Smart animations and transitions were introduced to create a smoother user flow, while component variants allowed for efficient prototyping and consistency across screens. Despite challenges in managing complexity and maintaining clarity, the project demonstrates how thoughtful UI/UX design can transform a functional system into a seamless user experience. Ultimately, the app reframes the refrigerator as not just an appliance, but an intelligent system—one that supports users’ daily habits through structured, intuitive, and responsive design.
